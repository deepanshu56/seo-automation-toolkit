Data loss prevention (DLP) software is a set of tools that detects and blocks
the unauthorized movement of sensitive data, such as customer records, financial
information, or intellectual property, across endpoints, networks, and cloud apps.

DLP works by classifying data, monitoring how it moves, and enforcing policies
that allow, log, or block an action. For example, a policy might stop an employee
from emailing a spreadsheet of credit-card numbers outside the company.

Organizations adopt DLP to meet compliance requirements like GDPR and HIPAA, to
reduce the risk of insider threats, and to gain visibility into where regulated
data actually lives. Modern DLP increasingly integrates with cloud access security
brokers and data-security posture management tools.
